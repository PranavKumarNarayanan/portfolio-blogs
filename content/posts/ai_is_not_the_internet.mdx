Whenever there is a conversation of why AI is bad, and how it eats up your brain with no crumbs left, there is one common argument that comes against you (provided that someone is actually sensible and convinced to talk about AI):

> AI is just like how internet was to us in the late 20th century. It's a tool.

And you know what AI is? It can be a lot, but one thing for sure. It's surely **not a tool**

But before we get into why AI is not a tool, and how people have completely misunderstood the meaning of a tool, let me tell you why I hate AI. If you are expecting a scientific approach to why I hate AI, there are a lot of articles out there that would educate you. I wanna talk about what is the first feeling I get when I see someone using AI, or when someone asks me to use AI to do a job. 

# Why I hate AI
Let's set one thing straight. By the phrase "I hate AI", I don't hate the technology, nor the upsurge in AI tools. I just simply hate the way people rely on it, and call it a tool.

## Creativity
The biggest reason I hate AI is for the sheer impact it makes in one's creative mind. Our brain is designed in a way that pondering is the only way to attain knowledge. Now how do we ponder? We search for information, we put some effort into searching them, and when we do find the answer we are looking for, we feel a sense of reward. 
This happens well when people used to search books in libraries to know something. 

This sense of reward is what that makes the knowledge we gained to persist. This sense of reward makes sure that it was worth the effort, and it would be a shame not to understand/memorize whatever we have learnt. 

This process of learning is completely obliterated by the usage of AI. 

When we use AI to search for even the simplest of the things, we completely remove this very sense of reward from our learning process. One can draw similarity between this and spoiled kids. When things are handed over to them in a silver spoon, they tend to neither value it, nor utilize it to the fullest. 

## Theft
AI is a thief, and you cannot prove me otherwise. A more precise term would be to call AI the "gang leader". The gang leader of a robbery gang does one thing. They make you steal. And AI does nothing better than that. 

Go to a university and see the projects made by students across the campus. Every single idea sounds too good to be true. Alzheimer detection, Breast cancer detection, Exam preparation website (if you are from my Uni you would know what I'm talking about ;)).

Now let's analyze these projects from two aspects. 

1. Firstly, I would go by a very rude yet true way. How can a 2nd year or a 3rd year computer science student, who knows little about Computer Science, let alone medicine, create a full fledged project about Alzheimer and breast cancer? 

    The point I'm trying to make here is, one would think the same when you see a poor youngster in the street driving a Ferrari Testarossa. The only way he could get it is by stealing. Nothing short of that happens with these "state-of-the art" projects made by these students.

2. Secondly, take these same projects, and run them through the internet. You would find a gazillion of these projects out there (some were again AI generated, but that makes it even easier to complain about), made by several other people. 

    The reason is simple. AI can itself, never think of something that never existed in the first place. It cannot create something new on its own. All it can do is to mash up unrelated topics into one idea and make it sound like it's the next groundbreaking invention the Nobel Prize committee is waiting to present award to.

The same is reflected in actual products made using AI. Several web services lack the basic sense of security, privacy, code conventions and maintainability because AI "assumed" that it's not necessary. It was busy crafting a neon-blue themed frontend with senseless animations that it forgot about the need for clean and safe code.

## Lack of learning
To extrapolate my first reason for hating AI, one would easily conclude that people who use AI extensively never learnt anything. In contrast to what people argue, saying that AI is such a good "tool" for learning, if you actually go and test them, you would know what I'm talking about. 

People who supposedly learn by AI, never really learnt anything. They just memorized a few points of the slop vomited by their Max Pro Ultra LLM model. They never really understood the concept. Instead, they assumed they understood it because the AI gave a ridiculous real world explanation making it sound so simple and intuitive.

# Why AI is not a tool?
The reason AI is not a tool is simple. It just doesn't adhere to the definition of a tool.
> A tool is something that lets you do a job that is practically impossible. The ease of doing a job is not the concern of the tool.

This is something that most of the people I interact with got wrong.

A tool's job is to ensure only two things. 
1. It should make an impossible job, possible to carry out.
2. The work done using that tool should be better than what the person would have done if he had done in manually.

And the most import thing of all is that, these two points are not mutually exclusive.

The reason an axe was made is that it is humanly impossible to cut a tree. And if it all a human manages to cut it with bare hands, or a stone, the logs cut by the axe are much better and much cleaner.

The same logic applies when we look at internet. It is humanly impossible for a person in Australia to access the files in Lithuania within 5 seconds. Even if they did, it surely would not be 5 seconds. This was solved by the internet. 

Now take the case for AI. Is it impossible for someone to write a blog manually? (am I a God to write my blogs completely by myself?) And even if they think AI does it more efficiently, can you assure me that the content generated by AI is actually useful? You simply cannot. 

A tool is not made to make your life easier. It was made to complete a job that would rather be impossible by human capabilities. AI does not do anything that a human cannot. 

So before you use AI to write your assignments or send a mail, think. Think if you are really that incompetent that you cannot write a few paragraphs of content without relying on AI.
